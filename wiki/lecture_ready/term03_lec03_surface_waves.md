# Term 3 Lecture 03 — Surface Waves

## One-line summary

Surface waves as both signal and noise: Rayleigh and Love wave physics, dispersion, multimodality, array-based dispersion measurement, inversion to S-wave profiles, and adaptive subtraction for ground-roll removal.

## Key concepts covered

- Rayleigh and Love waves: particle motion, energy trapping, and why they dominate land seismograms.
- Dispersion: phase velocity vs. frequency, group velocity, and the two-layer model intuition.
- Multimodality: fundamental and higher modes, depth sensitivity, mode misidentification, and multi-mode inversion.
- MASW/SWI workflow: acquisition, dispersion-curve extraction, forward modelling, and regularized inversion.
- Array analysis: f–k, slant-stack, high-resolution Radon, and f–k-MUSIC for resolving multimode dispersion.
- Practical pitfalls: low-velocity layers, LVL-guided waves, near-field effects, aliasing, and non-uniqueness.
- Adaptive subtraction: modelling ground roll from a dispersion curve and removing it with a local Wiener-like filter.
