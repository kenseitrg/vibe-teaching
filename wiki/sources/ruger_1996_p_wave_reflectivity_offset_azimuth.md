# Rüger (1996) — Variation of P-wave reflectivity with offset and azimuth in anisotropic media

Rüger, A. (1996). "Variation of P-wave reflectivity with offset and azimuth in anisotropic media." *SEG Technical Program Expanded Abstracts*, 1810–1813. https://doi.org/10.1190/1.1826488

## Contribution

Extends the symmetry-plane AVO results for HTI (horizontal transverse isotropy) media to arbitrary azimuth and large incidence angles. Shows that the P-wave AVO gradient varies with the squared cosine of the azimuthal angle, providing a physical basis for AVAz (amplitude versus azimuth) analysis of fractured reservoirs.

## Key points for teaching

- In HTI media, the AVO gradient depends on azimuth as $G(\phi) = G_\text{iso} + G_\text{aniso} \cos^2\phi$, where $\phi$ is the azimuth relative to the symmetry axis.
- The azimuthal variation can be inverted to find the symmetry-plane directions and a combination of the shear-wave splitting parameter and the anisotropy coefficient.
- At large incidence angles (greater than ~20°), additional anisotropy coefficients influence the reflection coefficient, helping to separate fracture parameters.
- The results are extended to orthorhombic media, which are more realistic models for fractured reservoirs.
- Linearized approximations work well for small elastic contrasts and weak anisotropy but fail at interfaces with large contrasts and strong anisotropy.
