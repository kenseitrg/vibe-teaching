# Carvajal, Fernandez & Aristimuno (2023) — Well tie tutorial and its importance in seismic interpretation and inversion

Carvajal, C., Fernandez, J., & Aristimuno, J. (2023). "Well tie tutorial and its importance in seismic interpretation and inversion." *GeoConvention 2023*, Calgary, Canada.

## Contribution

A practical tutorial on seismic-to-well ties, emphasizing a quantitative approach. Explains the Roy-White methodology as a coherence-matching technique and shows how wavelet choice and time-depth errors affect simultaneous inversion and elastic/geomechanical property estimation.

## Key points for teaching

- A well tie is a critical step for reservoir characterization; visual matching alone is insufficient.
- A quantitative approach estimates the wavelet's amplitude, phase, and frequency and tests their consistency.
- The Roy-White method is a coherence-matching technique that scans a volume around the well for the best statistical fit and provides error bars on the wavelet spectrum.
- Goals of a well tie include:
  - a reasonable match between synthetic and seismic at the objective level,
  - a calibrated time-depth relation for the starting model,
  - offset- or angle-dependent wavelets for inversion.
- Well ties should be repeated after seismic conditioning because processing changes the time-depth relation and wavelets.
- Using different wavelets in simultaneous inversion can bias the estimated elastic and geomechanical properties.
