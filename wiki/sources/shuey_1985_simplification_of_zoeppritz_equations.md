# Shuey (1985) — A simplification of the Zoeppritz equations

Shuey, R. T. (1985). "A simplification of the Zoeppritz equations." *Geophysics*, 50(4), 609–614.

## Contribution

Derives a simple, three-term approximation of the P-wave reflection coefficient as a function of incidence angle. The approximation separates the response into an intercept $R_0$, a gradient $G$, and a higher-order term $F$. It is the foundation of modern AVO analysis.

## Key points for teaching

- The reflection coefficient at angle $\theta$ can be written as $R(\theta) = R_0 + G \sin^2\theta + F \sin^2\theta \tan^2\theta$.
- The intercept $R_0$ is the normal-incidence reflection coefficient.
- The gradient $G$ is controlled by the contrast in Poisson's ratio and the impedance contrast.
- For many practical cases, the higher-order term is dropped, giving the two-term approximation $R(\theta) \approx R_0 + G \sin^2\theta$.
- The intercept-gradient crossplot is a standard tool for lithology and fluid interpretation.
