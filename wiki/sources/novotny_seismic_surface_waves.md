# Seismic Surface Waves

Novotny, O. (1999). *Seismic Surface Waves*. Lecture notes for post-graduate studies, Universidade Federal da Bahia, Salvador, Brazil.

## Main message

These lecture notes provide a self-contained theoretical introduction to seismic surface waves: body waves, Rayleigh waves on a homogeneous half-space, Love waves in a layered medium, and the matrix methods used to compute dispersion in multilayered media.

## Contents and key ideas

- Body and surface waves: differences in amplitude decay, geometric spreading, and dispersion.
- Rayleigh waves in a homogeneous isotropic half-space: derivation from elastic potentials, free-surface boundary conditions, and the Rayleigh polynomial. The phase velocity is non-dispersive in a homogeneous half-space.
- Love waves in a single layer over a half-space: constructive interference of SH waves, frequency-dependent dispersion relation, and the existence of higher modes.
- Rayleigh waves in a layer over a half-space: dispersion appears when the medium is vertically heterogeneous.
- Matrix methods for layered media: propagator / Thomson–Haskell formulations for Love and Rayleigh waves.
- Dispersion in general media: group and phase velocity, wave packets, and the band structure of surface waves.
- Examples of structural studies: how surface waves are used to infer Earth structure.

## Relation to lecture notes

Novotny provides the mathematical backbone for the lecture: the derivation of Rayleigh and Love waves, the layered-medium dispersion relation, and the concept of higher modes. It is useful for students who want to see where the dispersion equation comes from.
